# Daily Update — July 17, 2026

*Written for non-technical stakeholders. Plain language, no jargon.*

## In one line

The Appointments view got two upgrades: attorneys can now focus on just their own schedule, and each appointment shows the client's SharePoint documents right next to the notes. Both are built and tested; they go live on the next deploy.

---

## Appointments: focus on one attorney

Until now the Appointments view always showed every attorney side by side (the "big picture"). Now there's a simple switch at the top:

- **Overview** — all attorneys, as before.
- **One attorney (WH / M / LB / R)** — that attorney's schedule only, full width.

The choice is remembered and can be shared via the page link. Switching is instant — it just re-focuses what's already loaded, so there's no waiting.

---

## Appointments: documents next to the notes

Opening an appointment now shows the client's **SharePoint documents on one side and the notes on the other**, in the same window. The attorney can browse, preview and (if needed) upload a document without leaving the appointment.

This reuses the file browser built this week — including a small back-end tweak so the client's own e-file link (not just the ones inherited from case boards) shows up here. On the current data, that means the document panel is populated for the large majority of appointments that have a linked client.

---

## A note on testing

While testing locally we saw an empty Appointments screen — which turned out to be correct, not a bug: the local copy of the data is from July 2, and the default view is "Today," which had nothing scheduled (the next appointment in that data is July 20). On the live server, where the data syncs nightly, "Today" reflects the real day. (Idea noted for later: when a day is empty, automatically show the upcoming appointments instead of a blank screen.)
