# Daily Update — July 22, 2026

*Written for non-technical stakeholders. Plain language, no jargon.*

## In one line

Everything built over the past week is now consolidated in one place — including two features that landed today: cases parked in "North Pole" now come back on their own, and staff can generate a client's Word document straight from the dashboard. One deploy brings the whole lot to the server.

---

## "North Pole" cases can no longer be forgotten

Last week we found that cases parked in **North Pole** were quietly staying parked: of 8 parked cases, 4 had a return date that had already passed (one by 320 days) and 1 had no return date at all. "Temporarily hidden" was turning into "permanently forgotten."

That's now fixed, and it works the way we agreed:

- A parked case is hidden from the working board **only while its return date is still in the future**.
- **When the date passes, the case comes back on its own** — nobody has to remember to un-park it.
- **A case parked without a return date is never hidden.** This is deliberate: it makes "hidden forever" impossible, and nudges whoever parks a case to say when it should return.
- A parked case with a real deadline **still raises its deadline alert**. Parking tidies the work list; it never silences a legal deadline.
- A small toggle shows the parked cases on demand, each labelled with its return date.

---

## Generate a client document from the dashboard

Staff can now produce a client's Word document (e.g. the client letter) directly from the dashboard: it pulls the client's current data from Monday.com, fills the template, and downloads the file. Nothing is stored on the server, and each generation is recorded in the audit log.

---

## Tightened administrator access

The checks that restrict admin-only screens (user list, role changes, the audit log) were moved to a **single gate** in front of those routes, instead of being repeated inside each one. Same protection, but now a future admin screen can't accidentally ship without it.
